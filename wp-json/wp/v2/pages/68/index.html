ECIR 2020 workshops provide a platform for presenting novel ideas and research results in a focused and interactive way. Workshops can be of either a half-day (3 hours plus breaks) or a full day (6 hours plus breaks). Workshops are encouraged to be as dynamic and interactive as possible and should lead to a concrete outcome, such as the publication of a summary paper and\/or workshop proceedings.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Workshop proposals should contain the following information:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

  • Title, and abstract of the workshop;<\/li>
  • Motivation, and relevance to ECIR;<\/li>
  • Workshop goals\/objectives coupled with desired outcomes;<\/li>
  • Format\/structure of the workshop: planned activities, tentative schedule of events etc.;<\/li>
  • Intended audience, including how participants will be selected\/invited;<\/li>
  • Duration: a full day or half day workshop;<\/li>
  • Resources: what resources will be needed to deliver the workshop;<\/li>
  • Indicate if the workshop is related to or follows on from another workshop; if so, please, identify which conference it was previously held at, the past attendance and outcomes, and why another workshop is needed;<\/li>
  • Organisers with brief bio (2-3 sentences) of each with respect to the content of the workshop;<\/li>
  • Any other relevant information to support your proposal (e.g. programme committee, list of invited speakers, overall vision\/approach etc.)<\/li>
  • Workshop proposals will be reviewed by the workshop committee. A summary of the workshop will be published in the conference proceedings.<\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n

    All proposals should be submitted electronically through the conference submission system and must be in English.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Deadline for submission of workshop proposals is 1st September 2019, 11:59 pm (AoE), with notification of acceptance on October 1, 2019.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Workshop Chairs (workshops_at_ecir2020.org)<\/strong><\/h4>\n\n\n\n

    Suzanne Little
